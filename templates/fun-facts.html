Fun Facts

  • Many national coats of arms use animals (eagles, lions) to symbolize strength and sovereignty.
  • Some coats of arms include historical tools or plants that denote local industry or agriculture.
  • Heraldic colors have specific meanings: gold for generosity, blue for loyalty, red for bravery.
  • Some modern states retain monarchic symbols in their coats of arms even after becoming republics.
  • The last Emperor of Rome died fighting The last emperor of the Ottomans died in exile.
  • Many national emblems were standardized in the 19th and 20th centuries as nation-states formed.
  • Leaders' personal coats of arms historically displayed family lineage, claims, and alliances.
